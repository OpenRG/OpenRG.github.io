Matthew Jensen

Matthew Jensen is an OpenRG Principal working in information security research and tax policy.

Jensen monitors federal information security. His research seeks to understand what federal information insecurity means for policymakers in government and the people and organizations who are affected.

As a tax modeler, Jensen developed custom data sharing arrangements with the IRS, organized a team to synthesize publicly-sharable tax data from controlled sources, and advised projects to develop synthetic data and research validation servers. Working with controlled data, he organized and led modeling teams to estimate the revenue impact of policy changes. He also led teams that built web applications for general users to interact with the modeling software and controlled-source data.

Jensen's professional roles have included:

  • Principal, Open Research Group, 2022-Present
  • Board of Directors, Open Research Group Inc., 2017-Present
  • Advisory Board, Synthetic Data & Validation Server Project, Tax Policy Center, 2021 - Present
  • Founding Director, Open Source Policy Center at the American Enterprise Institute, 2013-2022
  • Expert, Mission to China on Tax Policy, International Monetary Fund, 2020-2021
  • Fellow, U.S. Chamber Foundation, 2012-2013
  • Research Assistant and Associate, American Enterprise Institute, 2010-2013
He is also co-creator of several notable open-source projects including:
  • IRS Disclosures
  • Tax-Calculator
  • Policy Simulation Library
  • Compute Studio.
His work and writing have been featured in The Wall Street Journal, The New York Times, The Washington Post, CNN, Fox News, MSNBC, and professional publications such as TaxNotes, The Bond Buyer, and the US Chamber Foundation's Business Horizons Quarterly.

Jensen graduated from Pomona College with a degree in math. He grew up in Indiana and lives in New Jersey with his wife and two children.
